Simple Macaroni Recipe

"Craving a quick and easy meal?
This classic macaroni recipe is perfect for a comforting, homemade dish in no time!"

Preparation Time

  • Total Time: 25 minutes
  • Preparation Time: 10 minutes
  • Cooking time 15 minutes

Ingredients

  • Macaroni(2 cups) The base of this dish, giving you that classic pasta texture.
    Choose your favorite pasta shape for variety!
  • Cheddar (1 cup, shredded) For that rich,
    gooey cheesiness that makes macaroni irresistible.
  • Milk (1 cup) This adds creaminess and helps blend all the ingredients together.
  • Butter (2 tablespoons) A bit of butter gives the sauce a smooth, silky texture.
  • Flour (2 tablespoons) Helps thicken the cheese sauce to a perfect consistency.
  • Salt and Pepper (to taste) Simple seasonings to bring out the flavors of the dish.
  • Optional: Paprika or Parsley (for garnish) A dash of paprika or a sprinkle of parsley can add color and a bit of extra flavor.

Instructions

  1. Boil the macaroni in salted water until al dente (about 8-10 minutes). Cooking the pasta just right ensures it's firm and won't get mushy when mixed with the sauce.
  2. In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat and whisk in the flour to form a roux. Stir constantly for 1-2 minutes to cook off the raw flour taste while keeping the mixture smooth.
  3. Slowly add the milk to the roux, whisking constantly until the sauce thickens.This step is key to making a creamy base for your cheese sauce. The milk should be added gradually to avoid lumps.
  4. Stir in the shredded cheese until fully melted and smooth. The cheese will melt into the sauce, creating that delicious, creamy texture.
  5. Season the sauce with salt, pepper, and any optional spices. Taste as you go and adjust the seasoning for the perfect balance of flavor.
  6. Drain the cooked macaroni and mix it with the cheese sauce. Gently toss everything together to evenly coat the pasta in the creamy, cheesy goodness.
  7. Serve immediately, garnished with paprika or parsley if desired. This dish is best enjoyed fresh and warm, so dive in while it's still hot!
